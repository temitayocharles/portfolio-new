# InfraForge Visual Direction Brief

## Purpose

This brief defines the premium visual language for the InfraForge brand hub and future launch assets.

The site should feel like a credible product/company surface, not a generic AI portfolio or noisy dashboard demo.

## Brand feel

- Premium technical product.
- Dark, precise, restrained, and cinematic.
- Infrastructure-grade rather than consumer-gimmick.
- Clear proof before claims.
- High-trust operator-console influence without clutter.

## Visual principles

### 1. Problem-solution clarity

Every visual should help explain a problem, an operating loop, or a proof artifact. Avoid decorative AI imagery that does not clarify the product story.

### 2. Restraint over spectacle

Use small glows, thin lines, measured spacing, and a controlled palette. The brand should feel expensive and deliberate.

### 3. Systems without over-explaining

Diagrams should show relationships at a product level. They should not expose every internal implementation detail unless the viewer has intentionally opened a case study.

### 4. Proof-led storytelling

Screens, diagrams, runbooks, and architecture artifacts should support the claim that InfraForge builds real systems: governed AI, model operations, operational intelligence, secure infrastructure, and education products.

## Palette

Primary background:

- Charcoal: `#080d12`
- Deep panel: `#0a0f14`
- Elevated panel: `#101419`

Accents:

- Amber: `#f59e0b`, `#fbbf24`
- Teal: `#14b8a6`, `#2dd4bf`
- Slate text: `#94a3b8`, `#cbd5e1`, `#f8fafc`

Avoid using blue as a dominant AI cue. Blue can appear only as a minor system-neutral shade if necessary, not as the brand mood.

## Typography and layout

- Large, calm headlines.
- Short, proof-oriented paragraphs.
- Cards with enough breathing room.
- Avoid dense technical walls on the homepage.
- Use monospaced labels sparingly for credibility, not decoration.

## Asset types

### Product-system diagrams

Use for:

- InfraForge product ecosystem.
- ForgeWatch/Iris/Jerry relationship.
- AI Inference Lab model-operations flow.
- Vault Ops trust boundary.

Rules:

- Use amber/teal linework.
- Limit labels to product roles and proof signals.
- Prefer calm topology over complex network spaghetti.

### Product mockups

Use for:

- Iris personal assistant workspace.
- Jerry mobile companion surface.
- ForgeWatch operational signal review.

Rules:

- Use realistic UI surfaces, not fake holograms.
- Do not overpromise features that the repositories do not support.
- Keep copy aligned with product boundaries.

### Short motion clips

Use later for:

- Hero background loops.
- Launch posts.
- Product explainers.

Rules:

- 5 to 8 seconds.
- Low motion, premium, dark, cinematic.
- No cartoon robots, generic AI orbs, or neon-blue tunnel effects.

## Current proof assets

The repository already includes architecture PNG/SVG assets for InfraForge, Project Iris, Jerry, ForgeWatch, AI Inference Lab, OpenLeaf, and Vault Ops. Phase 5 adds brand-system SVGs that can be reused in the site and launch materials.

## Launch-quality definition

A visual asset is launch-ready when it:

- Explains the system without over-explaining the implementation.
- Looks premium at desktop and mobile sizes.
- Does not rely on generic blue AI imagery.
- Supports a problem-solution story.
- Can be reused in social posts, case studies, and the brand hub.
