## Post 2: AI Inference Lab

AI models are easy to demo.

They are harder to operate.

That is the reason behind AI Inference Lab: a model-operations environment focused on LLM serving, runtime packaging, benchmarking, metrics, and capacity planning.

The work is not just about getting a model to respond. The real questions are:

- How is the runtime packaged?
- How is latency measured?
- What happens under concurrency?
- What metrics prove the system is healthy?
- How does the workload move toward GPU-backed capacity?
- How does the lab become repeatable instead of improvised?

AI Inference Lab is the InfraForge proof point for model operations: turning model serving into a measurable, observable, capacity-aware system.

## Post 3: Project Iris

Project Iris is not an engineering assistant.

It is a governed personal AI assistant for everyday digital work.

That distinction matters.

The problem Iris addresses is not only technical. It is the daily fragmentation of modern work: email, calendar, browser tasks, research, notes, follow-ups, documents, decisions, and long-running tasks.

The product direction is useful delegation with user control:

- memory
- approvals
- tools
- MCP
- email
- calendar
- browser workflows
- artifacts
- audit
- long-running tasks

The goal is not to make AI act mysteriously in the background. The goal is to make personal AI useful while preserving control, traceability, and trust.

## Post 4: ForgeWatch, Iris, and Jerry

One of the important ideas inside InfraForge is separation of roles.

ForgeWatch is not Iris.
Jerry is not ForgeWatch.
Iris is not an engineering dashboard.

They solve different problems:

- ForgeWatch watches infrastructure signals, alerts, incidents, KeepHQ events, Kubernetes state, and service health.
- Iris helps with governed personal AI workflows: memory, approvals, email, calendar, browser tasks, research, and long-running work.
- Jerry gives lightweight mobile access for commands, status, approvals, alert review, and assistant interaction.

That separation keeps the ecosystem clearer.

Operational intelligence, personal AI assistance, and mobile AI interaction should connect, but they should not collapse into one vague chatbot.

## Post 5: Platform engineering angle

The longer I build with AI, the more obvious this becomes:

AI systems need platform discipline.

Not just prompts. Not just wrappers. Not just demos.

They need:

- delivery paths
- observability
- secrets boundaries
- approval gates
- runbooks
- metrics
- runtime packaging
- auditability
- rollback paths
- platform ownership

That is the platform-engineering instinct behind InfraForge.

The public brand hub now shows the ecosystem through that lens: model operations, operational intelligence, governed personal AI, mobile interaction, education products, and the infrastructure foundation behind them.
